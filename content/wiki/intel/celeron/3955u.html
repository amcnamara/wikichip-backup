The Intel Celeron 3955U is a dual-core 64-bit mobile microprocessor released by Intel in the third quarter of 2015. The 3955U is designed to replace the Broadwell-based celeron. Manufactured using 14nm process, the Skylake-based 3955U Celeron processor can be configured to run at down to 10 Watt TDP. This processor, just like its predecessor lack support for any of Intel's advanced technologies such as hyper-threading, trusted execution, transactional synchronization extensions (TSX), and turbo-boost.
