Coffee Lake E (CFL-ER, Coffee Lake E Refresh) is the name of the core for Intel's workstations and entry-level servers line of processors based on the Coffee Lake microarchitecture serving as a successor to Kaby Lake DT. These chips are primarily targeted towards entry-level workstations and servers. Coffee Lake E processors are fabricated on Intel's enhanced 14nm++ process and introduced hexa-core parts. Coffee Lake E parts are branded as part of the Xeon E family.

Overview[edit]

Coffee Lake E based processors are a 2-chip solution - the microprocessor and the chipset. Coffee Lake E are Socket LGA-1151 and use Xeon-class Cannon Point chipset (i.e., C246). The microprocessor is connected to the chipset via 4 of the chip's 20 PCIe lanes using Intel's proprietary Direct Media Interface 3.0 (DMI 3.0), allowing for 8 GT/s transfer rate per lane.

Common Features[edit]

All Coffee Lake E processors have the following:

  • Dual-channel Memory
    • Up to DDR4-2666
    • Up to 64 GiB
    • Has ECC support
  • 16x PCIe (4 of the 20 are used by the bus as described above)
  • Quad/Hexa-core with 4 to 12 threads (not all E models support Hyper-Threading)
  • Everything up to AVX2 (SMM, FPU, NX, MMX, SSE, SSE2, SSE3, SSSE3, SSE4.1, SSE4.2, AES, AVX, FMA3, and AVX2)
  • Has support for Turbo Boost 2.0, vPro, VT-x, VT-d/EPT, TSX, Secure Key, Software Guard (SGX), Memory Protection (MPX), OS Guard, TXT
  • Graphics
    • UHD Graphics P630 (Gen9.5 GT2)
    • Base frequency of 350 MHz
    • Burst frequency of 1.15-1.2 GHz
