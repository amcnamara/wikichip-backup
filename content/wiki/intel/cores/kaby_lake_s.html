Kaby Lake S (KBL-S) is the name of the core for Intel's mainstream performance line of processors based on the Kaby Lake microarchitecture serving as a successor to Skylake S core. These chips are primarily targeted towards desktop performance to value computers, AiOs, and minis. Kaby Lake S processors are fabricated on Intel's enhanced 14nm+ process and provide slight enhancements over comparable Skylake models.

Overview[edit]

Kaby Lake S based processors are a 2-chip solution - the microprocessor and the chipset. Kaby Lake S are Socket LGA-1151 and use Union Point chipset (HUB) but they may also use previous generation (Skylake S) Sunrise Point via a firmware upgrade. The microprocessor is connected to the chipset via 4 of the chip's 20 PCIe lanes using Intel's proprietary Direct Media Interface 3.0 (DMI 3.0), allowing for 8 GT/s transfer rate per lane.

Common Features[edit]

All Kaby Lake S processors have the following:

  • Dual-channel Memory
    • Up to DDR3L-1600, DDR4-2400
    • Up to 64 GiB
  • 16x PCIe (4 of the 20 are used by the bus as described above)
  • 2 to 4 core with 2 to 8 threads (not all S models support Hyper-Threading)
  • Everything up to SSE4.2 (SMM, FPU, NX, MMX, SSE, SSE2, SSE3, SSSE3, SSE4.1, SSE4.2, AES) (not all S models support AVX2)
  • Graphics
    • HD Graphics 610 (Gen9.5 GT1) or HD Graphics 630 (Gen9.5 GT2)
    • Base frequency of 350 MHz
    • Burst frequency of 1-1.15 GHz
