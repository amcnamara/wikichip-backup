Apollo Lake (APL) is the name of the core for Intel's generation of system on chip serving as a successor to Braswell. These chips primarily targeted towards entry level 2-in-1 devices, low-cost laptops and desktops, and All-in-One PCs. Apollo Lake chips are manufactured on a 14 nm process and is part of Intel's the Goldmont microarchitecture.

Features[edit]

  • Base frequency of 1.1 GHz - 2.0 GHz
  • Burst frequency of 2.2 GHz - 2.6 GHz
  • 8 GB Memory, DDR3L-1866 / LPDDR4-2400
  • HD Graphics 500 / 505
  • 9th generation LP graphics
  • GPU base frequency of 200 MHz - 250 MHz
  • GPU burst frequency of 650 MHz - 800 MHz
  • GPU with 12 or 18 execution units
