Coffee Lake HR (CFL-HR, Coffee Lake H Refresh) is codename for Intel's line of performance mobile processors based on the Coffee Lake microarchitecture serving as a successor to Coffee Lake H. These chips are primarily targeted towards ultimate mobile performance, mobile workstations, and performance embedded devices. Coffee Lake HR processors are fabricated on Intel's enhanced 14 nm process.

Overview[edit]

Coffee Lake HR based processors are a 2-chip solution - the microprocessor and the chipset. Coffee Lake H are BGA-1440 and use 300-series chipset (HUB). The microprocessor is connected to the chipset via 4 of the chip's 20 PCIe lanes using Intel's proprietary Direct Media Interface 3.0 (DMI 3.0), allowing for 8 GT/s transfer rate per lane.

Common Features[edit]

All Coffee Lake HR processors have the following:

  • Dual-channel Memory
    • Up to 64 GiB
    • Up to DDR4-2666, LPDDR3-2133
  • 16x PCIe (4 of the 20 are used by the bus as described above)
  • 4 to 8 core with 2 threads per core
  • Everything up to AVX2 (SMM, FPU, NX, MMX, SSE, SSE2, SSE3, SSSE3, SSE4.1, SSE4.2, AES, AVX, AVX2)
  • Graphics
    • UHD Graphics 630 (Gen9.5 GT2)
    • Base frequency of 350 MHz
    • Burst frequency of 1.05 - 1.2 GHz
