Skylake S (SKL-S) is the name of the core for Intel's mainstream performance line of processors based on the Skylake microarchitecture serving as a successor to Devil's Canyon. These chips are primarily targeted towards desktop performance to value computers, AiOs, and minis. Skylake S processors are fabricated on Intel's 14 nm process and provide a considerable number of changes from the Broadwell/Haswell models.

Overview[edit]

Skylake S based processors are a 2-chip solution - the microprocessor and the chipset. Skylake S are Socket LGA-1151 and use Sunrise Point chipset (HUB). The microprocessor is connected to the chipset via 4 of the chip's 20 PCIe lanes using Intel's proprietary Direct Media Interface 3.0 (DMI 3.0), allowing for 8 GT/s transfer rate per lane.

Common Features[edit]

All Skylake S processors have the following:

  • Dual-channel Memory
    • Up to DDR3L-1600, DDR4-2133
    • Up to 64 GiB
  • 16x PCIe (4 of the 20 are used by the bus as described above)
  • 2 to 4 core with 2 to 8 threads (not all S models support Hyper-Threading)
  • Everything up to AVX2 (SMM, FPU, NX, MMX, SSE, SSE2, SSE3, SSSE3, SSE4.1, SSE4.2, AES, AVX, FMA3, and AVX2) (not all S models support FMA3, AVX and AVX2)
  • Graphics
    • HD Graphics 510 (Gen9 GT1) or HD Graphics 530 (Gen9 GT2)
    • Base frequency of 350 MHz
    • Burst frequency of 1-1.15 GHz
