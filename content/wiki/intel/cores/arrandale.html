Arrandale (also Arrandale DC) is the core name given by Intel for their dual-core mainstream x86-64 microprocessors using 5 series chipset. These were first generation (Westmere-based) Core i7 mainstream processors. These chips were manufactured on a 32 nm process and uses PM55 Express chipset. Arrandale servers as a successor to Clarksfield and extended the series to mobile Core i3 and Core i5 families.

Features[edit]

  • 32 nm process
  • PM55 Express chipset (Calpella platform)
  • 2 Cores, 4 Threads
  • 18 W - 35 W TDP
  • 2.5 GT/s DMI 1.0
  • 8 GiB Maximum Memory (DDR3-800/1066)
  • Support everything up to SSE4.2 (SMM, FPU, NX, MMX, SSE, SSE2, SSE3, SSSE3, SSE4.1, SSE4.2, and TXT). Except for Celeron which only supports up to SSSE3
  • Some models support AES as well
  • Supports TBT 1.0, and TXT
  • some models support vPro, VT-d, VT-x, EPT, and Hyper-Threading as well
  • HD Graphics (Ironlake)
