Coffee Lake R (CFL-R, Coffee Lake Refresh) is the name of the core for Intel's mainstream performance line of processors based on the Coffee Lake microarchitecture serving as a successor to Coffee Lake S. These chips are primarily targeted towards desktop performance to value computers, AiOs, and minis. Coffee Lake R processors are fabricated on Intel's 3rd generation enhanced 14nm++ process. Coffee Lake R is the first series of mainstream desktop PC octa-core microprocessors from Intel.

Overview[edit]

Coffee Lake R based processors are a 2-chip solution - the microprocessor and the chipset. Coffee Lake R are Socket LGA-1151 and use 300-series chipset (HUB). Despite using the same socket, those chips are not backwards-compatible with Union Point (or Sunrise Point). The microprocessor is connected to the chipset via 4 of the chip's 20 PCIe lanes using Intel's proprietary Direct Media Interface 3.0 (DMI 3.0), allowing for 8 GT/s transfer rate per lane.

Common Features[edit]

All Coffee Lake R processors have the following:

  • Dual-channel Memory
    • DDR4-2400-DDR4-2666
    • 64 GiB
  • 16x PCIe (4 of the 20 are used by the bus as described above)
  • 4 to 8 core with 4 to 16 threads
  • Everything up to AVX2 (SMM, FPU, NX, MMX, SSE, SSE2, SSE3, SSSE3, SSE4.1, SSE4.2, AES, AVX, FMA3, and AVX2)
  • Graphics
    • UHD Graphics (Gen9.5) 610 (GT1), 630 (GT2)
    • Base frequency of 350 MHz
    • Burst frequency of 1-1.2 GHz
