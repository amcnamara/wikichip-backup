Skylake H (SKL-H) is the processor core for Intel's line of performance mobile processors based on the Skylake microarchitecture, succeeding the Broadwell H core. These chips are primarily targeted towards ultimate mobile performance, mobile workstations, and performance embedded devices. Skylake H processors are fabricated on Intel's 14nm process and and provide a large set of improvements over comparable Broadwell models.

Overview[edit]

Skylake H based processors are a 2-chip solution - the microprocessor and the chipset. Skylake H are Socket BGA-1440 and use Sunrise Point chipset (HUB). The microprocessor is connected to the chipset via 4 of the chip's 20 PCIe lanes using Intel's proprietary Direct Media Interface 3.0 (DMI 3.0), allowing for 8 GT/s transfer rate per lane. Some high-end Skylake H processors (i.e., those with Iris Pro Graphics 580) incorporate a large 128 MiB of side cache on-package.

Common Features[edit]

All Skylake H processors have the following:

  • Dual-channel Memory
    • Up to DDR4-2133
    • Up to 64 GiB
  • 16x PCIe (4 of the 20 are used by the bus as described above)
  • 2 to 4 core with 2 to 8 threads (not all H models support Hyper-Threading)
  • Everything up to AVX2 (SMM, FPU, NX, MMX, SSE, SSE2, SSE3, SSSE3, SSE4.1, SSE4.2, AES, AVX, AVX2) (Except for Celeron which does not have AVX/AVX2)
