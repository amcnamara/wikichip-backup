Matisse (MTS) is codename for AMD's mainstream through high-end desktop (HEDT) microprocessor line based on the Zen 2 microarchitecture serving as a successor to Pinnacle Ridge. Matisse processors are fabricated on TSMC 7 nm process.

Matisse-based microprocessors are branded as 3000-series Ryzen 3, Ryzen 5, Ryzen 7, and Ryzen 9 processors.

Overview[edit]

Matisse processors are the successor to Pinnacle Ridge, fabricated on a 7 nm process based on the Zen 2 microarchitecture. Those processors are a complete system on a chip with both the northbridge and southbridge on-chip. Matisse chips offer 16 PCIe Gen 4.0 lanes (generally for the GPU) along with four additional x4 PCIe lanes for SATA.

Common Features[edit]

All Matisse processors have the following:

  • Dual-channel Memory
    • Up to DDR4-3200
    • Up to 128 GiB
  • Up to 16 cores / 32 threads
  • 65 W / 105 W
  • Everything up to AVX2 (i.e., SMM, FPU, NX, MMX, SSE, SSE2, SSE3, SSSE3, SSE4.1, SSE4.2, AES, AVX, FMA3, and AVX2), and SHA
  • Precision Boost 2, SMEP, 2-way SMT, AMD-Vi, XFR 2 ?
