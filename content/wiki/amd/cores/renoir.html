Renoir is codename for AMD series of mainstream mobile and desktop APUs based on the Zen 2 CPU and Vega GPU microarchitectures succeeding Picasso. Renoir processors are fabricated on TSMC 7 nm process.

Overview[edit]

Renoir is an SoC for the mobile segment and mainstream desktop and workstation based on the Zen 2 microarchitecture incorporating a Vega GPU.

Common Features[edit]

All Renoir processors have the following:

  • Mem: Up to:
    • 64 GiB of dual-channel DDR4 memory up to 3200 MT/s
    • 32 GiB of quad-channel LPDDR4x memory up to 4266 MT/s
  • I/O: 16 PCIe lanes (1x8 for GPU, 1x4 for storage, and 1x4 for additional peripherals)
  • Up to 8 cores / 16 threads
  • TDP: 15 W / 35 W / 45 W TDP
    • Configurable TDP-down of 10 W and a TDP-up of 25/35/54 W
  • ISA: Everything up to AVX2 (i.e., SMM, FPU, NX, MMX, SSE, SSE2, SSE3, SSSE3, SSE4.1, SSE4.2, AES, AVX, FMA3, and AVX2), and SHA
  • Precision Boost 2, 2-way SMT
  • Graphics
    • Radeon Vega 11/10/8/6/3 (Vega)
    • Up to 1.6 GHz

Die[edit]

Main article: Zen 2 § Die

Renoir microprocessors are fabricated on TSMC's 7-nanometer process. This SoC integrates 9.8 billion transistors on a single 156 mm² monolithic die which includes both the Zen 2 CPU cores along with the Vega GPU and various other additional components.

  • 7-nanometer process (N7)
  • 9,800,000,000 transistors
  • 156 mm² die size
