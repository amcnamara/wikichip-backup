Whitehaven is codename for AMD's highest-performance enthusiasts microprocessors based on the Zen microarchitecture. Fabricated on GlobalFoundries' 14 nm process, Whitehaven-based processors are branded as Ryzen Threadripper.

Overview[edit]

Whitehaven are a series of high-performance desktop processors designed by AMD based on their Zen microarchitecture. Those processors have 64 PCIe lanes with 4 permanently reserved for the chipset and the other 60 generally set up as 48 lanes for GPUs/accelerators and 12 for I/O.

Common Features[edit]

All Whitehaven processors have the following:

  • 60 PCIe lanes
  • Quad-channel Memory
    • Up to DDR4-2666 ECC
    • Up to 1 TiB
  • Up to 16 cores / 32 threads
  • Everything up to AVX2 (i.e., SMM, FPU, NX, MMX, SSE, SSE2, SSE3, SSSE3, SSE4.1, SSE4.2, AES, AVX, FMA3, and AVX2), and SHA
